## What Needs to Change

Add the "Accepted compromises" end-of-run summary section to both orchestrator SKILL.md files (near-identical prose):

- **`/quo-fix-issue`:** extend Section 7's per-Issue summary (`## Issue [x] of [total] done:`, ~line 477–480) with the new "Accepted compromises" section. Because the tracker is session-scoped (it accumulates across the whole run/batch per SR-3.3), the section reflects the tracker file's current contents at the moment the summary renders.
- **`/quo-execute`:** extend the run-end summary surface (Section 7 "Final Output" ~line 570, and/or Section 9 "Output Final Summary" / `## Bee Execution Complete:` ~line 608 — place it at whichever surface is the run-end summary, analogous to where `/quo-fix-issue` renders it; keep the two placements analogous so the prose stays near-identical).
- **Rendering (SR-5.3):** read the tracker file at the documented path (Epic `po`'s convention), iterate its entries, and render **one bullet per entry** showing: the finding, the chosen path (the Decision), the rationale, and the follow-up Issue ID (or `none`).
- **Empty/absent case (SR-3.4):** when the tracker is empty (no compromises accepted during the run) OR the tracker file does not exist for the current run, the section is **OMITTED ENTIRELY** — do NOT render an empty heading, "N/A", or "no compromises" placeholder.
- **Volume edge case (SR-6.5):** when the tracker accumulates more than ~10 entries, surface them ALL in full (no truncation), with a short prologue noting the volume.

## Why

When a compromise is legitimately accepted during a run, it must remain visible after the run ends rather than baked silently into the new baseline. The end-of-run "Accepted compromises" surface is the user-facing read of the tracker — it lets the user immediately see which compromises were accepted. Omitting the section when empty keeps the common-case summary clean.
